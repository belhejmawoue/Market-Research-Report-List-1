<p><p>The parboiled rice market can be categorized into two main types: white parboiled rice and brown parboiled rice. White parboiled rice undergoes steaming and drying before milling, resulting in a softer texture and quicker cooking time. It retains more nutrients than regular white rice. Brown parboiled rice, on the other hand, retains its bran layer, offering higher fiber content and a nuttier flavor. Both types appeal to health-conscious consumers, with white parboiled rice favored for its convenience and brown for its nutritional benefits.</p></p>
