<p><ul><li>Vector-based Cas</li><li>DNA-free Cas</li></ul></p>
<p><p>CRISPR technology involves gene editing using CRISPR-associated (Cas) genes, which serve as molecular scissors to modify DNA in specific ways. The market for CRISPR and Cas genes can be categorized into vector-based Cas, where plasmids or viral vectors deliver the Cas proteins and guide RNAs into target cells, and DNA-free Cas, which utilizes ribonucleoprotein complexes that do not integrate into the genome. Both approaches are essential for applications in therapeutics, agriculture, and research, each offering distinct advantages in gene editing precision and safety.</p></p>
