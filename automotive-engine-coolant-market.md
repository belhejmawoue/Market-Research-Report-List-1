<p><ul><li>Ethylene Glycol</li><li>Propylene Glycol</li><li>Glycerin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive engine coolant market features three primary types: ethylene glycol, propylene glycol, and glycerin. Ethylene glycol is widely used due to its effective heat transfer and freeze protection properties, though it is toxic and requires careful handling. Propylene glycol serves as a safer alternative, suitable for environments where non-toxicity is essential, though it may have lower thermal efficiency. Glycerin, derived from natural sources, is considered eco-friendly and biodegradable, appealing to environmentally conscious consumers while offering adequate performance in cooling systems.</p></p>
