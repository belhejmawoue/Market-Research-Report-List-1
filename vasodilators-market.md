<p><p>The vasodilators market comprises three primary types: arterial dilators, venous dilators, and mixed dilators. Arterial dilators target the arteries, reducing systemic vascular resistance and improving blood flow to organs, often used in hypertension and heart conditions. Venous dilators primarily affect veins, decreasing venous return and alleviating conditions like heart failure. Mixed dilators simultaneously act on both arteries and veins, offering comprehensive treatment for various cardiovascular issues. Each type serves distinct therapeutic purposes based on patient needs.</p></p>
