<p><ul><li>Superconducting Magnet</li><li>Permanent Magnet</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The MRI equipment market primarily consists of two types of magnets: superconducting magnets and permanent magnets. Superconducting magnets, commonly used in clinical MRI machines, provide high magnetic field strength, enabling detailed imaging of tissues. They require cooling to extremely low temperatures, making them more expensive and complex to maintain. In contrast, permanent magnets offer a more affordable and portable alternative, ideal for lower-field MRI applications. Although they provide lower image resolution, they are easier to operate and require minimal maintenance.</p></p>
