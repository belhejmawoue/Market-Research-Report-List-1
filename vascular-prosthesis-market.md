<p><p>The vascular prosthesis market includes various types of grafts used for vascular surgeries. Polyester grafts are durable and commonly used for arterial bypasses. ePTFE (expanded polytetrafluoroethylene) grafts offer excellent biocompatibility and are often used in peripheral procedures. Polyurethane grafts provide flexibility and compliance, making them suitable for dynamic applications. Biosynthetic grafts combine synthetic materials with biological components, promoting better integration with surrounding tissues. Each type serves specific clinical needs, enhancing patient outcomes in vascular interventions.</p></p>
