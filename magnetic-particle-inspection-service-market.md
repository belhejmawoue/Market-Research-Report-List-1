<p><p>The Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI) service market is divided into two primary types: the Wet Method and the Dry Method. The Wet Method involves suspending magnetic particles in a liquid medium, allowing for effective application on complex geometries and extensive surface areas, enhancing visibility of defects. In contrast, the Dry Method utilizes dry magnetic particles, typically in powder form, which are sprinkled onto the surface. This method is advantageous for environments where liquid contamination is a concern, offering ease of cleanup and less mess. Both methods are essential for nondestructive testing in various industries.</p></p>
