<p><ul><li>Synchronous Motor</li><li>Asynchronous Motor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The traction motor market for railcars primarily features two types: synchronous and asynchronous motors. Synchronous motors operate at a constant speed, aligned with the supply frequency, providing high efficiency and torque at varying loads, making them ideal for high-speed trains. In contrast, asynchronous motors, or induction motors, run at variable speeds and are robust and reliable, perfect for urban transit systems. Both motor types contribute to the flexibility, efficiency, and performance of railcar traction systems.</p></p>
